# SIM-108: EuroKorea Gateway × 레이첼 김 (교포 2세)

## 시나리오

Rachel Kim is a Korean-American architect visiting Korea for the first time in over a decade. She's searching for an English-language app that provides deep cultural and historical content about Jeonju and Namwon — her parents' hometowns. She finds EuroKorea Gateway through a travel blog that listed it among "multilingual Korea apps." She downloads it hoping for English audio guides with architectural depth. Opening the app, she sees only Russian, Spanish, French, and German options. No English. As someone who values depth of information above all else, and whose daughter needs child-appropriate English content, this app is immediately disqualifying. She spends a few minutes browsing the photo gallery — the Jeonju hanok images are genuinely beautiful — before concluding this cannot serve her needs.

---

## 사용자 여정

### 1단계: 진입
- Travel blog recommendation for "multilingual Korea apps" leads to download
- Opens app expecting English audio guides with historical/architectural depth
- Language screen: RU/ES/FR/DE only — no English

### 2단계: 행동
- Explores photo gallery in Spanish UI (closest to navigable without understanding)
- Finds high-quality Jeonju hanok photos — appreciates the visual curation
- Looks for audio guide feature — finds it, but only in the four European languages
- Checks for child-appropriate content for her 10-year-old daughter — none found
- No heritage/genealogy content, no architectural depth information

### 3단계: 결과
- Uninstalls. Returns to English-language Korea travel blogs and Korea Times
- Notes: "Beautiful photos, wrong language, wrong depth for what I need"

## Pass/Fail 판정

> **❌ FAIL**

Rachel Kim's needs — English-language deep historical and architectural content, child-appropriate explanations, and heritage-focused itineraries — are entirely outside EuroKorea Gateway's scope. Without English support, even the app's audio guide feature, which would be Rachel's primary use case, is inaccessible. 13 points, FAIL.

## 개선 제안

1. Adding English as a supported language would immediately unlock the Korean-American diaspora market — a high-spending, culturally motivated segment that is currently underserved
2. Deep architectural and heritage audio guide content (beyond surface-level descriptions) would differentiate from competitors and serve diaspora visitors like Rachel who want scholarly depth
