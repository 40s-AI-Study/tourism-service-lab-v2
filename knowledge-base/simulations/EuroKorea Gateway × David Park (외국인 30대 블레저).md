# SIM-105: EuroKorea Gateway × David Park (외국인 30대 블레저)

## 시나리오

David is a Korean-American PM who relies heavily on English-language apps for travel in Korea. He discovers EuroKorea Gateway through a Reddit post on r/koreatravel where someone mentioned it as a multilingual Korea app. He downloads it hoping to find English-language audio guides and attraction info. However, the language selection screen only shows Russian, Spanish, French, and German — no English. As a PM himself, he immediately recognizes the product-market fit issue: the app is built for European language speakers, not English speakers. He spends two minutes exploring the visual gallery before uninstalling.

---

## 사용자 여정

### 1단계: 진입
- Reddit r/koreatravel post mentions "multilingual Korea tourism app"
- Downloads expecting English audio guides for Gyeongju and Seoul
- Opens app: language selection shows RU/ES/FR/DE only — no English option

### 2단계: 행동
- As a PM, immediately diagnoses: "This is a European-language-specific product"
- Browses the visual gallery — high-quality photos of Gyeongju and Jeju are impressive
- Checks if any content is in English — none found
- Notes the absence of real-time crowd info, time-based itinerary recommendations, and English audio guides he specifically needs

### 3단계: 결과
- Uninstalls after 3 minutes. Posts on Reddit: "Heads up — EuroKorea Gateway is for Russian/Spanish/French/German speakers only, not English"
- Returns to Google and TripAdvisor for English content

## Pass/Fail 판정

> **❌ FAIL**

EuroKorea Gateway does not support English, which is David Park's primary language for app navigation. His core needs — English audio guides, time-optimized itineraries, and accurate attraction data — are entirely unmet. As a high-standard PM, he would not use an app that lacks the language he needs. 13 points total, FAIL.

---

## 개선 제안

1. Add English as a fifth language option — English-speaking visitors are a massive segment and English would serve as a bridge language for many non-European foreign visitors as well
2. Clarify app store listing to explicitly state "for Russian, Spanish, French, and German speakers" to set correct expectations and reduce irrelevant downloads
