# SIM-109: EuroKorea Gateway × 아미나 사이드 (말레이시아 무슬림)

## 시나리오

Amina is a Malaysian Muslim travel influencer planning her first Korea trip. She actively searches for Korea travel apps and finds EuroKorea Gateway through an Instagram post. The high-quality photos of Korean landmarks catch her eye, and she downloads it. As a digital marketer fluent in English, she quickly navigates the language selection screen — but finds only Russian, Spanish, French, and German. No English, no Malay, and critically, no halal filter or prayer room locator anywhere in the app. These two features are non-negotiable for Amina. She spends a few minutes admiring the photo gallery for potential content inspiration, then uninstalls.

---

## 사용자 여정

### 1단계: 진입
- Instagram ad showing Korea travel photos leads to App Store download
- Opens app as experienced digital marketer — immediately evaluates language options
- Language screen: RU/ES/FR/DE — no English, no Malay

### 2단계: 행동
- Attempts navigation in Spanish UI (most familiar Roman script she can guess)
- Photo gallery: high-quality images are genuinely inspiring for her Instagram content
- Searches for halal filter, prayer room (musalla) locator — features do not exist
- Checks if there is any Muslim-friendly travel tag or filter — none
- Realizes this app has zero Muslim travel infrastructure

### 3단계: 결과
- Saves a few photos for reference, then uninstalls
- Posts in her Malaysian Muslim Korea travel WhatsApp group: "Nice photos but no halal info, skip"
- Returns to HalalTrip app and English-language halal Korea travel blogs

---

## Pass/Fail 판정

> **❌ FAIL**

Amina's non-negotiable requirements — halal restaurant filter, prayer room locator, and English UI — are entirely absent from EuroKorea Gateway. The app is designed for a completely different user segment. As an influencer, her negative word-of-mouth in the Malaysian Muslim travel community could actively discourage others from downloading. 12 points, FAIL.

---

## 개선 제안

1. Adding English as a supported language would immediately open the app to the enormous Southeast Asian Muslim travel market, which is one of the fastest-growing Korea tourism segments
2. A halal-friendly layer (restaurant filter, prayer room pins) would require minimal additional API work and would serve millions of potential users the existing app completely ignores
